In continuation of last week's focus, we refined our process for measuring and drilling holes into the thin bar segments that will become the sliding components of our system. Initially, we used a digital caliper to measure hole location, marked the location with an automatic spring-loaded punch, then drilled the holes while keeping the bar clamped in a vice. Due to the hole markings being too small, we struggled to gauge whether the drill actually aligned with our measurements, and we had a lot of asymmetry within our first bar. To fix this, we reinforced each punch marking with a marking from a larger punch, which was large enough for the drill head to be guided by it. Eventually, we struggled to fit a rod through these revised holes as well, so we improvised for a hand drill to create a clear path through the bars. <br> <img src="./bars.jpeg" alt=bars/jpeg" width="400"/> <br> <br> Another focus we took on was drilling slider ridges into our base bar and evaluating how tightly the small bars fit inside. Ideally, we hoped that a bar could match the slider perfectly with no misfit, because this would maximize conductivity between them and minimize energy waste.
